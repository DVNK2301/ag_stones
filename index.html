Granite

Granites are undoubtedly the most popular stone type used in countertop applications today. Granite is an extremely durable natural stone that transcends many styles, whether it be transitional, rustic, or contemporary. Granite is an excellent choice for kitchen countertops, flooring, and other heavily trafficked surfaces due to its hardness, low absorption, and resistance to high temperatures.

Marble

Marble is traditionally prized for its aesthetic appeal, accentuated by pronounced veining and often bold colors. Marble was once limestone that achieved metamorphosis from intense pressure and high temperatures within the Earth. This altered its make-up and introduced other minerals that produce the valuable colors and veining found in Marble today.

Porcelain

Porcelain stone is a high-quality ceramic material composed of natural clays, feldspar, and other minerals. This results in a material that boasts remarkable strength, resistance to wear, and a luxurious appearance reminiscent of natural stone.

Quartz

Quartz slabs are engineered to perfection, combining natural quartz crystals with resins, polymers, and pigments. This fusion results in a non-porous surface that resists stains, scratches, and the wear and tear of daily life. Choose quartz for a surface that effortlessly withstands the demands of both residential and commercial spaces.

Quartzite

Quartzite emerges as a metamorphic rock originating from Sandstone, characterized by its dense composition and natural Quartz grain content. The formation of Quartzite occurs when Sandstone undergoes a transformative process upon encountering deeply buried magma on the Earth's surface.
